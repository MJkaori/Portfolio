About Me

I began learning web development during my senior year of high school because my major was ICT. Since then, I've come to appreciate creating websites, even when it occasionally leads to frustration due to technical challenges. However, when things do work out, I experience delight and excitement. The more new things I learn, the more eager I am to put them into practice. When I started college, my intention was to enroll in the IT program. Unfortunately, I ended up majoring in Information and Communication Technology with a Bachelor of Business Technology and Livelihood Education, which is related to the program I desired in some way.

I haven't had as much time to continue improving my web development skills since I started college. However, the web development course I took in my third year of college reignited my passion for web development. During my fourth year of college, I had the opportunity to gain practical experience through an internship (practice teaching). Additionally, in my ninth-grade ICT class, I even had the chance to teach HTML. After earning my degree, I delved deeper into front-end web development and even enrolled in an online course for front-end developers. Every time my code works exactly as I intended, it brings me immense joy.

Since I was still a child, I have loved engaging in various forms of art, including painting, watercolor, and art crafts. Now, I have also ventured into digital arts. I even created a webtoon series titled 'The Forest God' under the pen name Satella_ei. The pandemic provided me with the opportunity to dedicate more time to practicing my art style and experimenting with new mediums, such as animation. For my webtoon and 2D animation practice, I have been using Clip Studio Paint EX.
